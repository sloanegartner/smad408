App Ideas

App Ideas // Default Setting Considerations

Last year I came up with an app called "Dolley". It was food app for JMU students where you could transfer punches, flex, or dining between other students. My group instead decided to go with SHERPA. I think it would still be fun to develop the app or at least make a low/high fidelity prototype of it. Also, in relation to making apps, in the beginning of the year when we read "Technically Wrong", we learned about default settings. It's important to keep in mind default settings that could be inconsiderate to people. For example, with Bitmoji, I think it would be a good idea to have the option to display your gender. To make a Bitmoji you can only choose from male and female instead of just Bitmoji. I think it would be more inclusive to add the option of mentioning your gender/sexuality and then taking away the male/female default. The book also mentioned being more inclusive with race and ethnicity.

barMe

barMe // UX/UI Independent Project 2020

I'm working on this app with another student in class. We are basically doing another SMAD 317 project. We have all the research, we are now in the process of making low/high fidelity prototypes, wire-framing, and coming up with the look of the interface. Below is the process of the logo. I like the color scheme from the start, but it was the logo that was giving me trouble. The first two were suppose to be location points as beer, but they ended up looking like ice cream cones. Then the second one with the bar stools, I felt like there was too much happening. We are now working with the latest logo, which is a location point and a martini. I like this one because it looks professional and works with what the app does, finds bars close to the user. I'm keeping the color scheme and am now working on the XD portion.
